3-Day Tsavo East & West Safari

PRICE USD 775 per person
DURATION 3 Days / 2 Nights
TOUR TYPE Safari
DESTINATIONS Tsavo East & West

Safari Overview

Explore Kenya's oldest and largest national park on this captivating 3-day safari through Tsavo East and Tsavo West. Together forming one of the world's largest national parks, Tsavo offers an authentic wilderness experience with diverse landscapes ranging from endless plains to volcanic hills and natural springs.

Famous for its iconic "red elephants" that dust themselves with red soil, Tsavo is home to massive elephant herds, lions (including the legendary man-eaters of Tsavo), buffaloes, and diverse wildlife. Discover Mzima Springs' crystal-clear waters teeming with hippos and crocodiles, explore the dramatic Shetani Lava Flows, and visit the Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary in your quest to spot the Big Five in their natural habitat.

Safari Highlights

  • See Tsavo's famous "red elephants" - massive herds dust-bathing in red soil
  • Learn about the legendary man-eaters of Tsavo - historical lion attacks of 1898
  • Visit Mzima Springs with underwater viewing chamber for hippos and crocodiles
  • Explore Shetani Lava Flows - dramatic volcanic landscapes from ancient eruptions
  • Visit Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary - protected area for endangered black rhinos
  • Spot diverse wildlife: giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, gazelles, and abundant birdlife
  • Experience varied terrain: open savannahs, rocky outcrops, volcanic hills, and riverine forests
  • Endless photography opportunities in Kenya's largest wilderness area

Important Notes

  • Climate: Tsavo is hot and dry. Temperatures can exceed 35°C. Bring sunscreen, hat, and light clothing.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water. Bottled water is provided during game drives.
  • Travel Time: Nairobi to Tsavo East is approximately 4-5 hours. Expect scenic drives with wildlife sightings.
  • Connectivity: Limited mobile network in the parks. Embrace the wilderness disconnection!
  • Dress Code: Neutral colors (khaki, brown, green) for game drives. Avoid bright colors and white.
  • Malaria: Tsavo is a malaria zone. Consult your doctor about prophylaxis before travel.
  • What to Pack: Binoculars, camera with telephoto lens, sunglasses, insect repellent, light jacket for early mornings.
  • Tipping: Budget USD 10-15 per day for guide, USD 5-10 per day for lodge staff.
  • Best Time: Year-round destination. Dry seasons (June-October, January-February) offer best wildlife viewing.

Detailed Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Nairobi / JKIA – Tsavo East

You will be picked up either from Jomo Kenyatta International Airport or your hotel/residence in Nairobi, then depart by road towards Tsavo East National Park, Kenya's oldest and largest park. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours through scenic countryside. Arrive at your lodge/camp in time for lunch.

In the afternoon, enjoy your first game drive in Tsavo East, famous for its large "red elephants" that dust-bathe in the park's distinctive red soil, giving them their unique coloring. The park's vast open savannahs and acacia woodlands provide excellent wildlife viewing opportunities.

Watch for lions, buffaloes, giraffes, zebras, and diverse birdlife against the backdrop of endless plains. The late afternoon light creates perfect conditions for photography as animals become more active.

Later, return to the lodge for dinner and overnight, often accompanied by the sounds of the African bush.

Accommodation: Selected lodge in Tsavo East (Full Board)

Meals: Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Red elephants, vast savannahs, first Big Five encounters

Day 2

Tsavo East – Tsavo West

After breakfast, check out and enjoy a morning game drive en route as you head to Tsavo West National Park. This transfer game drive offers excellent opportunities to spot wildlife while traveling between the two parks, maximizing your safari experience.

Arrive at your Tsavo West lodge in time for lunch and some relaxation. In the afternoon, embark on a game drive exploring the park's stunning and diverse landscapes, including:

  • Mzima Springs – Crystal-clear natural springs fed by underground rivers from the Chyulu Hills. Home to hippos and crocodiles, featuring an underwater viewing chamber where you can observe aquatic life.
  • Shetani Lava Flows – Dramatic black volcanic landscapes formed by eruptions just 200-500 years ago. The name "Shetani" means "devil" in Swahili, reflecting local legends.
  • Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary – A 90 sq km fenced sanctuary protecting Kenya's critically endangered black rhinos. One of Kenya's most successful rhino breeding programs.

Tsavo West's varied terrain includes volcanic hills, rocky outcrops, and riverine forests, creating diverse habitats for wildlife. The park offers more dramatic scenery compared to the open plains of Tsavo East.

Return to the lodge for dinner and overnight stay.

Accommodation: Selected lodge in Tsavo West (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Mzima Springs, Shetani Lava, Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary, volcanic landscapes

Day 3

Tsavo West – Nairobi / JKIA

Rise early for a final morning game drive to catch wildlife at their most active during the cooler morning hours. This is an excellent time to spot predators returning from nocturnal hunts and herbivores grazing in the dawn light.

Return to the lodge for a hearty breakfast, then check out and depart for Nairobi or JKIA. Enjoy scenic views along the journey as you leave the wilderness behind, with opportunities to stop for refreshments en route.

You will be dropped off at your hotel, residence, or the airport in time for your onward travel, carrying unforgettable memories of Kenya's largest wilderness area and the iconic red elephants of Tsavo.

Meals: Breakfast

Drop-off: Nairobi or JKIA (timing coordinated with your onward plans)

Highlights: Final wildlife viewing, scenic return journey

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (June to October & January to February): Best for game viewing as animals congregate around water sources. Roads are more accessible and wildlife easier to spot in thinner vegetation.

Wet Season (March to May & November to December): Lush green landscapes, migratory birds, and fewer tourists. The park is beautiful but some tracks may be challenging. Great for photography with dramatic skies.

Year-Round Destination: Tsavo's vast size means excellent wildlife viewing throughout the year, with each season offering unique experiences.
