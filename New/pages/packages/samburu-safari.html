Safari Overview

Discover the untamed beauty of Northern Kenya with our exclusive 3-day Samburu safari experience. Samburu National Reserve is a hidden gem, offering unique wildlife encounters in a rugged, semi-arid landscape dotted with doum palms and volcanic hills along the Ewaso Nyiro River.

This reserve is famous for hosting the "Samburu Special Five" - rare species found nowhere else in Kenya: the majestic Grevy's zebra, elegant reticulated giraffe, Somali ostrich, Beisa oryx, and the graceful gerenuk (giraffe gazelle). With fewer crowds than the southern parks, Samburu offers intimate wildlife encounters and authentic safari experiences, complemented by rich Samburu culture and stunning sunset vistas over the river.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1

Nairobi to Samburu National Reserve

Depart Nairobi in the morning by road or a scheduled flight to Samburu. If traveling by road, the journey takes about 6–7 hours through the central highlands and across the equator at Nanyuki. Along the way, guests enjoy scenic views of Mount Kenya and the Laikipia plateau.

Upon arrival at Samburu, check in at your chosen lodge or luxury tented camp overlooking the Ewaso Nyiro River, followed by lunch. In the late afternoon, head out for the first game drive in Samburu.

This reserve is renowned for its unique wildlife species, often called the "Samburu Special Five": Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, Somali ostrich, Beisa oryx, and gerenuk. The evening concludes with dinner under the stars.

Accommodation: Selected Lodge (Full Board)

Highlights: Equator crossing, Mount Kenya views, Samburu Special Five

Day 2

Full Day Game Drives in Samburu

The day begins with an early morning game drive, the best time to spot predators such as lions, leopards, and cheetahs before the heat of the day. Guests return for a hearty breakfast and some leisure time by the pool or terrace overlooking the river, where elephants often come to drink.

After lunch, enjoy an afternoon game drive, exploring the rugged terrain dotted with doum palms and volcanic hills. Samburu is less crowded than other parks, making it ideal for intimate safari experiences.

In the evening, guests return for a bush dinner accompanied by traditional Samburu dance and storytelling around the campfire.

Accommodation: Selected Lodge (Full Board)

Highlights: Early morning predator viewing, river wildlife, cultural performance

Day 3

Samburu to Nairobi

After an early breakfast, guests enjoy a final game drive as they exit the reserve, catching last glimpses of wildlife and landscapes.

If flying, transfer to the airstrip for the return flight to Nairobi. If traveling by road, the return journey allows for a stop in Nanyuki for shopping and a relaxed lunch before continuing to Nairobi.

Guests arrive in the late afternoon, marking the end of a memorable Samburu safari.
